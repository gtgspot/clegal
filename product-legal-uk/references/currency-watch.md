UK product / consumer protection law moves. The below lists the areas most likely to have changed since model training, with verify-at sources.

## Digital Markets, Competition and Consumers Act 2024 (DMCC Act)

**Commencement is phased — verify current status before relying on any provision being in force.**

- **Consumer protection provisions** (subscription contracts, drip pricing, fake reviews, CMA's direct enforcement powers): some provisions commenced April 2025; others in secondary legislation — verify current commencement orders at legislation.gov.uk.
- **Digital markets provisions** (Strategic Market Status, conduct requirements, pro-competition interventions for SMS firms): verify whether CMA has designated any SMS firms and what conduct requirements have been imposed.
- Verify at: legislation.gov.uk (commencement orders); gov.uk/cma (CMA DMCC Act guidance and enforcement).

## Online Safety Act 2023 (OSA 2023)

**Ofcom is issuing codes of practice in tranches — verify what's currently in force.**

- Category 1 and 2 services: Ofcom categorisation thresholds may have been updated — check current secondary legislation.
- Illegal content risk assessments: verify whether the relevant Ofcom codes of practice are in force for the relevant service category.
- Children's safety duties: Age Assurance and Children's Safety Codes — verify current in-force status.
- Fraudulent advertising provisions: check current status.
- Verify at: ofcom.org.uk (online safety); legislation.gov.uk (OSA 2023 commencement orders).

## ICO Enforcement and Children's Code

- **ICO Children's Code (Age Appropriate Design Code):** In force since September 2021 but ICO has issued updated guidance and taken enforcement action against specific products — check for current enforcement priorities and guidance at ico.org.uk.
- **ICO fines and enforcement notices:** Verify current enforcement posture and any notable recent decisions — these inform calibration of ICO risk in launch reviews.
- **UK GDPR Art 22 (automated individual decision-making):** ICO has issued updated guidance on AI and automated decisions — verify current position at ico.org.uk/for-organisations/uk-gdpr-guidance-and-resources/artificial-intelligence.
- Verify at: ico.org.uk/action-weve-taken (enforcement); ico.org.uk (guidance).

## FCA Consumer Duty and Financial Promotions

- **Consumer Duty (PS22/9):** In force for existing products from July 2023 and closed book from July 2024. Verify whether your specific product category has been subject to recent FCA review or thematic work.
- **Financial promotions (FSMA s 21):** FCA has updated the financial promotions regime, including for crypto assets — verify current requirements for the specific product category. Communicating an unapproved financial promotion is a criminal offence.
- **FCA financial promotions guidance:** Verify most recent version at fca.org.uk/publications/finalised-guidance.
- Verify at: fca.org.uk (supervisory statements, policy statements, financial promotions guidance).

## CMA Green Claims Enforcement

- **CMA Green Claims Code (2021):** CMA has conducted market reviews and issued guidance on specific sectors (fashion, food, household products). Check for any sector-specific investigations relevant to the product.
- **DMCC Act 2024 empowers the CMA** to take direct enforcement action against misleading green claims — verify commencement status and any enforcement actions.
- Verify at: gov.uk/cma-cases (CMA market studies and enforcement); asa.org.uk (ASA green claims adjudications).

## Product Safety Reform

- **Product Safety and Metrology etc Act 2024:** Enacted to reform GPSR — verify current commencement status. The GPSR 2005 may still be the operative regime for most products.
- **MHRA medical devices regulation:** Post-Brexit MHRA regime; UKCA marking deadlines — verify current timeline at gov.uk/guidance/medical-devices-conformity-assessment-and-the-UKCA-mark.
- Verify at: legislation.gov.uk; gov.uk/guidance/product-safety (OPSS); gov.uk/mhra (medical devices).

## AI Governance

- **EU AI Act:** Does not apply in the UK, but affects UK companies with EEA market presence. Verify current in-force provisions (prohibited practices from February 2025; high-risk AI provisions from August 2026). If the product serves EEA users, the EU AI Act may apply alongside UK rules.
- **UK AI governance:** The UK government is taking a sector-led / principles-based approach rather than a single horizontal AI Act (as of 2025). Check for any sector-specific guidance from CMA, FCA, ICO, or Ofcom on AI — each regulator is issuing AI-specific guidance in their domain.
- Verify at: gov.uk/government/publications (AI Opportunities Action Plan, AI guidance); ico.org.uk (AI guidance); fca.org.uk (AI in financial services).

## ASA / CAP Code Updates

- **ASA adjudications and CAP guidance:** The ASA publishes adjudications continuously — check asa.org.uk for recent decisions on claim types relevant to the product. AI-generated content, green claims, influencer marketing, and subscription advertising are active ASA enforcement areas.
- **CAP Code amendments:** Verify current version of the CAP Code at asa.org.uk/type/non-broadcast/code-rules.html.
- Verify at: asa.org.uk (adjudications, guidance); asa.org.uk/type/non-broadcast/code-rules.html (current CAP Code).
