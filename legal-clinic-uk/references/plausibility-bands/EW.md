# Plausibility bands — England & Wales (and UK-wide tribunal rules)

These are rough plausibility ranges, not computations. If a student-entered due date falls outside the range, the `/legal-clinic-uk:deadlines --add` flow flags it for re-check. The skill does **not** compute — it catches gross arithmetic errors in the student's own work. Every citation here is `[model knowledge — verify]` unless the supervisor has replaced it with a connector-retrieved or user-provided source.

## How to use

- One row per deadline type the clinic sees regularly.
- Typical range is a plausibility window, not a holding.
- Cite the governing rule in the Notes column so the student has somewhere to recompute against.
- Computation-of-time rules vary by court/tribunal; re-state them in Notes when relevant.

## Key computation-of-time rules (England & Wales)

- **County Court / High Court (CPR):** CPR r 2.8 (calendar days, exclude day of service), CPR r 2.9 (courts closed on that day: extend to next day court open). `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Employment Tribunal:** ET Rules 2013, r 4 (calendar days). The 3-month-less-one-day period runs from the effective date of termination (unfair dismissal) or date of act complained of (discrimination), not from the date of knowledge. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Tribunals (general):** Tribunal Procedure (First-tier Tribunal) rules: calendar days unless stated otherwise. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Service extensions:** CPR r 6.26 (deemed service timing), CPR r 6.27 (additional time for service by post / email). Check if limitation clock runs from cause of action or from service.

## Scotland — key differences

For clinics with Scottish matters, the main differences from E&W are:

- **Prescription:** Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973. The 5-year short negative prescription applies to most obligations (not the Limitation Act 1980). `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Housing (Scotland) Act 2006 / Private Housing (Tenancies) (Scotland) Act 2016:** private residential tenancy; notice to leave procedure differs from E&W s.21. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Employment Tribunal Scotland:** same UK ET rules apply; deadlines are identical to E&W. Court of Session for EAT appeals from Scotland.
- **Sheriff Court:** Simple Procedure (small claims equivalent) and Summary Cause procedure for debt/housing; response times differ from CPR. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Load a Scotland-specific band file** when the clinic has Scottish cases — this E&W file is not a substitute for Scottish procedural deadlines.

## Northern Ireland — key differences

For clinics with NI matters:

- **Industrial Tribunal (NI):** not Employment Tribunal (GB). Employment Rights (Northern Ireland) Order 1996. Same 3-month-less-one-day ET equivalent rule. NIHRC and ECNI for human rights / equality matters. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Limitation:** Limitation (NI) Order 1989 — broadly similar to Limitation Act 1980 but verify. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **County Court (NI):** different from County Court (E&W); NI Rules of Court. `[model knowledge — verify]`
- **Load a NI-specific band file** when the clinic has NI cases.

## Computation-of-time reminder

- **CPR (E&W courts):** CPR r 2.8 (calendar days, exclude day of service or act); CPR r 2.9 (courts closed on last day: extend to next day court open); CPR r 6.26 (deemed service dates vary by method).
- **Employment Tribunal (E&W):** calendar days; 3-month-less-one-day is computed strictly; ACAS early conciliation pauses the period (Employment Tribunals Act 1996, s 18A).
- **Tribunals (general):** check the applicable Procedure Rules for that tribunal — not all follow CPR r 2.8.
- **This band file is a plausibility check, not a substitute for the court/tribunal's own rule.** Always confirm against the applicable procedural rules and any specific directions in the case.
